pixlabel  v1.0

A web-tool for rapid and accurate custom labeling pixels

We built this tool to rapidly collect (by hand) massive amounts of ground-truth pixel-level data.

You can collect data for use in machine learning, fit logistic regression models, or inspect color thresholds.




Instructions

  • Step 1: Load an image from your local hard drive.
  • Step 2: Enter a label. Literally, it can be anything: plants, soil, rocks, elfs, etc. Make sure you don't misspell the label.
  • Step 3: Start clicking on the areas of the image that represent the label. pixlabel will record the timestamp, row, column, and RGBA (red, green, blue, alpha) values for a 3 by 3 square, taking the mouse pointer as the center pixel.
  • Step 4: Download collected data in .CSV format.

Important notes
  • As long as you don't refresh the page, pixlabel will keep track of the pixels even after loading a new image. So feel free to process as many images as you want.
  • Use as many labels as you want in the order you want. An empty label results in an empty entry in the output file.
  • The "clear" button removes the image in the canvas and all the records. To clear everything requires two clicks. You will see that the button changes color. This is to minimize the risk that you accidentally clear up all the records. After the second click there is now way to recover your data.
